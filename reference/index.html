Who's behind this

A short version

I'm a researcher who lives in two worlds that turn out to rhyme. In one, I record the brain: near-infrared light and scalp electrodes, then the long, careful work of turning noisy optical traces into something you can trust. In the other, I write mathematics that a computer checks line by line.

Both come down to the same instinct — take something that looks like intuition and make it rigorous. A clean preprocessing pipeline and a finished Lean proof scratch the exact same itch.

This site is my lab notebook, portfolio, and playground in one. Poke around.

Research

Reading the brain, honestly

The signals are small, the noise is large, and the interesting part is everything you do before the statistics. Here's what I spend my time on.

fNIRS

Preprocessing that survives review

Optical density → motion correction (TDDR, wavelet, spline) → modified Beer–Lambert → HbO/HbR, with short-separation channels regressing out systemic noise. Reproducible, documented, and boring in the best way.

Montage

Optimal optode layouts

Choosing source–detector placements that maximize cortical sensitivity to the regions I care about — while physically sharing the cap with EEG electrodes. A coverage-vs-constraints optimization, not a guess.

EEG

Two modalities, one head

Co-registering electrical and hemodynamic signals so they explain each other instead of fighting for scalp real estate.
